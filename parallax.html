Kangaroo Island

Still very wild!

Kangaroo Island is an island off the coast near Adelaide, South Australia.

With an area of 4405km2 and a population density of 1.07/km2, the island has about half the population density of areas such as Siberia and Western Sahara. And it shows! Outside of the main port town and a few other towns, you will be seeing vast amounts of untouched nature, as well as the scars of wildfires.

All images on this page are from my own trip to Adelaide and Kangaroo Island in particular in May 2023.

History

A long history of isolation.

The last known Aboriginal presence on the island disappeared over 2000 years ago, and between then and its early settlement in the 19th century and eventual permanent colonization in 1836, there was no human presence on the island as far as we know today.

Much like Australia in general, the island did and still has a wide variety of endemic flora and fauna that can't be found anywhere else. From the self-explanatory Kangaroo Island Kangaroo, to an unique species of honeybee that makes it extremely illegal to bring foreign honey or other bee-related products to the island.

Some Landmarks

Many sights to see!

There's many unique sights to see and things to experience on the island, and the local population will more than likely be eager to give you ideas!

There's the Remarkable Rocks, an unique collection of rock formations eroded out of solid rock over the last 500 million years.

There's the Admiral's Arch, a natural arch hollowed out by the often rough sea hitting the shore on that side of the island.

And there's also opportunities to get up close and personal to a colony of seals in Seal Bay, a honey farm that will do a good job at convincing you to bring some of their unique honey home and much more.

How to get there?

Enjoy the Fleurieu Peninsula on the way!

I can only speak for the route I took, but I'm very happy with it!

There's many other wonderful destinations along the Fleurieu Peninsula south of Adelaide while on the way to Port Jarvis, where you can take the ferry from.

Especially Port Elliot and the nearby Victor Harbor are wonderful calm seaside towns, with many options for a place to stay over the night as long as one orders in advance. And it's recommended, as the trip from Adelaide all the way to Port Jarvis is a long one!
